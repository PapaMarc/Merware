Here are a few details on my background, interests and experience from a water centric perspective. In general, I've always been drawn to the sea and sea life, and thus the activities on, around and under the water.

 

In short, I'd like to respond to a help wanted ad in SoFlo something along the lines of "Window Washer Wanted.  Must love fish, waving to the kids, and work well under pressure."

 

  • As an early teen,  I received a National Science Foundation grant in 1979 to study heavy metal concentrations at Marist College along the Hudson River.
  • I grew up sailing on the Great South Bay on Long Island, New York and raced Sunfish out of the Bellport Bay Yacht Club; in the early 80s I competed in the North Atlantic Regionals in NJ
  • I've been an avid diver with over 315 dives logged <as of May 21, 2004-- need to update more current tally.>, totaling ~214 hours bottom time (or ~9 days) in both fresh and salt waters, and cold and warm locations ranging from my my current home in Puget Sound and nearby British Columbia, to the warmer waters of Hawaii, Tahiti, Australia, Belize, Grand Cayman and South Florida.
  • For 8 years, I served as a volunteer diver at the Seattle Aquarium starting in Feb 1996, yielding ~350 hours of total service in their 40k gallon Pacific Coral Reef (PCR) tank washing the windows, vacuuming the gravel, feeding the fish, and waving to the kids, before i moved to Hong Kong in April 2004.  My supervisor Alan Tomita, and chief biologist for the aquarium's Exotics team Bill Robertson can be contacted as references regarding my work at the Seattle Aquarium and comfort in the water.
  • In '05 I completed my weekly rotation as a volunteer in Hong Kong's Ocean Park Atoll Reef 5 million liter (1.3m gal) exhibit. What a world class exhibit, amazing creatures (including hammerheads, mature grouper and spotted eagle rays, cownose rays, guitarfish, sturgeon, etc) and stellar crew i had the good fortune of working with (including David Lai curator OPHK who made my participation possible, volunteer coordinator Yvonne Lim, Walter Tang now the assistant curator & dive buddy Lell Luk among others could upon request provide comment on my time with them).

The group has also been a volunteer force in community events including the annual Bonnett House cleanup, Broward County IntraCoastal cleanup, the Pompano Pier cleanups, and Kayak Against Cancer.
